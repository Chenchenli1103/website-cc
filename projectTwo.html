‘GO STEPS’

A design of steps-teller & fitness App

Background information

The project: Fitness applications have opened the ground to monitor users’ activity records. However, some users try to “cheat” on the system to either get undeserved rewards or to build a positive image on social media. Our goal of this porject is to design a system that can effectively manage fitness app users’ cheating behaviors to promote more physical activities.

design process

We first identify what causes users’ cheating behaviors on fitness apps as well as their mental models of doing physical exercises in general through secondary research and user interview.

Next, we identify one problem that is considered the major cause for cheating.

Then we design a solution based on data gathered from previously conducted research.

Finally we perform a usability testing to identify usability issues with our prototype.

1. Competitive Analysis

We analyzed several fitness applications that are currently available in the market. We then grouped these apps into the following categorizations: gamification; fitness guidance; body data tracking. please click here to check detail analysis report if necessary.

GAMIFICATION

Gamification may only create short-term behavioral changes, which has little value to promoting a healthier lifestyle.

BODY DATA TRACKING

Users’ main purpose of using these apps is to monitor their activities, which provides minimum interaction with the application itself.

FITNESS Guidance

Apps of this kind usually serves as a go-to resource which will only be used when users want to do physical exercises.

2. primary research result

In primary research part, we conducted six interviews (n=6) with people who are currently using, or have experience using fitness applications to assist their physical exercises. Location Sharing

-

To share the workout places and locations of sports hall.

common achievement

-

To add social and game based features to the app.

3.1 why did we choose common achievement as our solution?

direcct to target

-

We hoped this solution could directly help solve lacking motivation and easy giving up. In such perspective we thought it better than Plan 2 and Plan 3 of information sharing.

teamwork

-

By providing others’ help, we hoped users would get more encouragement and sense of responsibility for persistance.

time saving

-

We just had two weeks to go through the whole design process, thus it would be better to think of a small-scale but in-depth solution.

easy to realize

-

Compared to Plan 1 the virtual assistant, we thought this solution easier for technology implementation .
